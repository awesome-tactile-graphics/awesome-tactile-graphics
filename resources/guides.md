This page curates **practical, standards-oriented references** for designing, evaluating, and producing tactile graphics.


## Core Standards

- **BANA Tactile Graphics Guidelines (TGG)**  
  *Braille Authority of North America’s official standard for tactile diagrams (maps, charts, STEM materials).*  
  **Covers:** symbol sets, textures, line weights, label conventions, scale, math/science figures.  
 [BANA TGG Manual](https://brailleauthority.org/tg/web-manual/index.html)  

- **Regional & National Standards**  
  Examples: [UKAAF Guidelines for Tactile Diagrams](https://www.ukaaf.org/standards/), [ICEVI European Standards](https://fondazioneroberthollman.it/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/KEY_ICEVI_web.pdf).  


## Design Heuristics 

- **Haptic Legibility Principles**  
  *Focus on minimalism, discriminable textures, contour hierarchy, spacing thresholds, and density limits for charts and maps.*  
  [Gardner, “Tactile Graphics: An Overview and Resource Guide” (1996)](http://itd.athenpro.org/volume3/number4/article2.html)  

- **STEM-Specific Conventions**  
  *Axis simplification, tactile-friendly markers, reduced clutter, clear layering.*  
[APH STEM Tactile Graphics Guide](https://sites.aph.org/files/manuals/7-30006-00.pdf)  


## Evaluation & Study Design

- **BLV-Centered Evaluation Metrics**  
  Consider both perceptual and cognitive factors:  
  - *Line Density Score* (vs. BANA thresholds)  
  - *Texture Discriminability* (forced-choice accuracy)  
  - *Recognition / Comprehension* (task success/time)  
  - *Workload & Usability* (SUS, NASA-TLX adapted)  
  [Stangl et al., “Designing While Blind” – ASSETS ’23](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3597638.3614546)

- **Accessibility Checklists**  
  [APH Tactile Graphic Evaluation Checklist](https://sites.aph.org/files/manuals/7-08851-00.pdf)
